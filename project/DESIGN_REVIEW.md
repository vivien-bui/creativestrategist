## BIGGEST WEAKNESSES

### 1. **The Zine Aesthetic Is Juvenile for a Senior Strategist** ⚠️⚠️⚠️

**The problem:** Washi tape. Handwritten notes. Decorative doodles. Playful margin annotations. Lowercase everything. This aesthetic works beautifully for a junior designer or indie studio. But for a *senior strategist* applying to Droga5, AKQA, or Wieden+Kennedy?

**The tension:** These agencies hire people to lead strategy, shape culture, make strategic decisions that move millions. The visual voice here says "I'm playful and creative" but whispers "maybe I'm too junior to lead."

Compare this to senior strategist portfolios at top agencies:
- **More confident.** Less apologetic.
- **More authoritative.** The design gets out of the way.
- **More data-forward.** Results, metrics, impact.
- **More strategic.** The work demonstrates thinking, not just taste.

**What a recruiter thinks:** "This is beautiful, but is this person ready to run a strategy team? Or are they still in the 'personality-driven' phase of their career?"

**The fix:** You don't need to abandon the aesthetic. But you need to *frame* it differently. The zine voice works if it's a deliberate *choice* backed by strategic insight. Right now it just feels... cute.

### 2. **No Clear Hierarchy of Work** ⚠️⚠️⚠️

You have 5 case studies. All seem equally featured. But which is your *best* work? Which demonstrates your highest level of strategic thinking?

**The problem:** A senior strategist would lead with their strongest case—the one that best demonstrates their methodology, insight, and impact. Instead, you present a grid of equal weight.

**What's missing:** Why should I care about Sharkbeauty over Doritos? What makes Cryoglow your signature work? The portfolio doesn't tell me.

**Better approach:** Feature ONE hero case study. Make it undeniable. Then show 4 supporting projects. This gives the portfolio narrative structure and demonstrates confidence.

### 3. **Case Studies Lack Strategic Depth** ⚠️⚠️⚠️

Looking at the detail pages (Cryoglow, etc.), each case study has:
- A one-sentence strategy insight
- Some imagery
- A few process bullets (Find, Build, Earn)
- Minimal actual thinking

**What's missing:**
- Why did this strategy work? (The insight)
- How did you validate the insight? (Research/data)
- What did it achieve? (Results/metrics)
- What would you do differently? (Self-awareness)

**Example of weak case study:**
> "Repositioning cold-tech skincare from clinical to covetable: a creator-first launch strategy that tripled the week-one sales target in AU + NZ."

**Example of strong case study:**
> "Research showed beauty creators saw skincare tech as 'clinical and intimidating.' We flipped the positioning by making them the experts—not scientists. Each creator got to interpret the tech through their own beauty lens. Result: 3x week-one sales target. More importantly: we built a creator ecosystem that drove 60% of month-two sales organically."

See the difference? One is a tactic. The other is *strategy.*

### 4. **No Evidence of Business Impact** ⚠️⚠️⚠️

**Critical gap:** Where are the numbers?
- How much did the campaign move the business?
- What metrics matter? (Sales? Brand lift? Engagement? Culture shift?)
- Did the work win any awards?
- What was the audience reach?
- How did this compare to the brief's KPIs?

A senior strategist *leads with impact.* Your portfolio is all craft, no results. To a recruiter, that suggests you're talented at design but uncertain about measurement and business outcomes.

### 5. **The "About Me" Section Doesn't Build Confidence** ⚠️⚠️⚠️

Your statement:
> "I find the cultural tension a brand can credibly own, then build the moment people can't help but share."

**What it tells me:** You think poetically about culture. ✓

**What it doesn't tell me:**

- Are you a brand strategist? Content strategist? Cultural strategist?
- What's your track record?
- Do you lead teams or work solo?

**Senior strategist profiles typically say:**
> "I work at the intersection of brand strategy and cultural insight. I help Fortune 500 brands find their authentic voice in moments that matter. I've led strategy on campaigns that reached 50M+ people and drove 40%+ brand lift. My approach: 1) Deep cultural diagnosis, 2) Authentic brand positioning, 3) Earned-media-first execution."

Yours is poetic. Theirs is credible.

### 6. **The Skills Section Undermines Your Credibility** ⚠️⚠️⚠️

**You tell me:**
