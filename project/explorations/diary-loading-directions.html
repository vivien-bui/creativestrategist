Direction 1 · Dear Diary. The wait is handwriting: an ink line writes itself across ruled paper with a red margin rule, ending on a handwritten caption. Quietest of the three, closest to the portfolio's existing voice, and cheap to build (one SVG stroke animation). Best if the diary is text-first.

Direction 2 · Nightstand. A bedside mood — dark ink stage, crescent moon, a small book riffling its pages until it "finds today." The serif date doubles as the loaded state's header, so the loader hands off seamlessly. Best if entries are date-anchored and you want an intimate, end-of-day feel.

Direction 3 · Desk Ephemera. Scrapbook logic: polaroids, a sage stamp, a ticket stub tumble onto the desk while a slightly crooked washi-tape progress bar fills. The most playful and the only one with explicit percent progress. Best if the diary is visual/photo-heavy.
