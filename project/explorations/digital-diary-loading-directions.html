Three digital-native directions

Round one leaned analog — ink, pages, ephemera. These three treat the diary as software: a draft being typed, entries syncing, a pocket device booting. Same Warm Gallery tokens throughout.

Direction 1 · Draft Autosave. The loader pretends the diary is mid-thought: a monospace line types itself out ("dear me, today felt like…"), a sage caret blinks, and an "autosaved just now" stamp ticks below. The wait reads as the diary composing itself. Rotating the typed phrases keeps repeat loads fresh.

Direction 2 · Sync. The most product-like: a small app window with traffic-light chrome, a shimmering skeleton of the sidebar and entry, and a live status bar ("Syncing 128 entries…"). The skeleton is the exact layout of the loaded app, so the transition is a cross-fade into real content — zero layout shift.

Direction 3 · Pocket Pixel. The diary as a beloved handheld: sage device shell, olive LCD, a pixel heart bouncing over a chunky block progress bar with a "Loading diary…" ticker. Nostalgic-digital rather than analog — Tamagotchi energy. The boldest personality of the three; best if the diary's voice is playful.
