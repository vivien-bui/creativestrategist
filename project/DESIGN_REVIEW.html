Executive Summary

The Verdict

Your portfolio is visually distinctive and crafted with genuine thoughtfulness. It has a clear point of view, consistent design system, and excellent technical execution.

But here's the hard truth: It reads as mid-level designer/strategist, not senior. The aesthetic is charming but slightly undercuts your credibility. The case studies lack strategic depth. There's no evidence of business impact. A recruiter at Droga5 or AKQA would appreciate the craft but question your strategic chops.

Overall Positioning: This portfolio gets you meetings at design-forward agencies (Instrument, Fantasy). It probably doesn't get you meetings at strategy-focused shops (R/GA, AKQA, Wieden+Kennedy, Special). That's a significant gap.

The problem: Washi tape. Handwritten notes. Decorative doodles. Playful margin annotations. Lowercase everything. This aesthetic works beautifully for a junior designer or indie studio. But for a senior strategist applying to Droga5, AKQA, or Wieden+Kennedy?

The tension:

These agencies hire people to lead strategy, shape culture, make strategic decisions that move millions. The visual voice here says "I'm playful and creative" but whispers "maybe I'm too junior to lead."

Compare this to senior strategist portfolios at top agencies:

  • More confident. Less apologetic.
  • More authoritative. The design gets out of the way.
  • More data-forward. Results, metrics, impact.
  • More strategic. The work demonstrates thinking, not just taste.

What a recruiter thinks: "This is beautiful, but is this person ready to run a strategy team? Or are they still in the 'personality-driven' phase of their career?"

2. No Clear Hierarchy of Work ⚠️⚠️⚠️

You have 5 case studies. All seem equally featured. But which is your best work? Which demonstrates your highest level of strategic thinking?

The problem: A senior strategist would lead with their strongest case—the one that best demonstrates their methodology, insight, and impact. Instead, you present a grid of equal weight.

What's missing: Why should I care about Sharkbeauty over Doritos? What makes Cryoglow your signature work? The portfolio doesn't tell me.

3. Case Studies Lack Strategic Depth ⚠️⚠️⚠️

Looking at the detail pages, each case study has:

  • A one-sentence strategy insight
  • Some imagery
  • A few process bullets (Find, Build, Earn)
  • Minimal actual thinking
Example of weak case study: "Repositioning cold-tech skincare from clinical to covetable: a creator-first launch strategy that tripled the week-one sales target in AU + NZ." Example of strong case study: "Research showed beauty creators saw skincare tech as 'clinical and intimidating.' We flipped the positioning by making them the experts—not scientists. Each creator got to interpret the tech through their own beauty lens. Result: 3x week-one sales. More importantly: we built a creator ecosystem that drove 60% of month-two sales organically."

See the difference? One is a tactic. The other is strategy.

4. No Evidence of Business Impact ⚠️⚠️⚠️

Critical gap:

Where are the numbers?

  • How much did the campaign move the business?
  • What metrics matter? (Sales? Brand lift? Engagement? Culture shift?)
  • Did the work win any awards?
  • What was the audience reach?
  • How did this compare to the brief's KPIs?

A senior strategist leads with impact. Your portfolio is all craft, no results. To a recruiter, that suggests you're talented at design but uncertain about measurement and business outcomes.
